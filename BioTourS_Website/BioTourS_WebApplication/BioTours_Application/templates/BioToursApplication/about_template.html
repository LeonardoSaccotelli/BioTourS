Project Summary

BioTourS aims at introducing best practices in sustainable cross-border tourism, tackling new challenges in environmental and marine safeguard as well as preserving the natural heritage of the Adriatic-Ionian area. The main goal is to involve youngsters in cross-border touristic activities, so as to raise awareness on cetacean conservation. By combining high technological tools with standardized survey techniques and direct involvement of youth in Citizen Science activities, BioTourS will improve knowledge on cetaceans which is currently heterogeneous and defective in the programme area. The main output is the creation of a Citizen Science tourism model, for expanding science knowledge on dolphin conservation and tourism management. Cross-border Youth Camps consisted in marine research activities for cetacean conservation using nautical equipment on board of Lead Partner (LP) Jonian Dolphin Conservation (JDC) Research Vessel “Il Porto di Taranto”. Cross-border Youth Camps further included educational and specialized workshops on land organized by LP in in each Project Partner territory whilst PPs were actively involved in logistic implementation in each Partner area.
BioTourS Project Partners are:

  • Agjencia Kombëtare e Turizmit (Albania)
  • Comune di Termoli (Italy)
  • Inovaciono preduzetnički centar Tehnopolis (Montenegro)

The activities involved 4 different target groups among youngsters (students, young researchers, people with disabilities, young tourism operators). For about a month the JDC research vessel operated in the South-Adriatic Sea in order to demonstrate, with the support and active involvement of Project Partners and local stakeholders, that it is possible to combine eco-tourism with direct scientific research on the topic of cetacean monitoring also in the area just as the JDC has been doing for a decade in the Gulf of Taranto with its citizen science program. The ultimate goal is sustainable tourism that respects and preserve the marine ecosystem.

Activities

Youth camps activities were carried out over a 25-day period (from 4th October to 29th October) where four target habitats were identified: the Adriatic coast of Bari; Termoli in Italy; the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro and Albanian coastlines. The camps focused on retrieving information (abundance, behaviour, group size, sighting location) on cetaceans using thematic equipment such as echo sounders, photo-video recording systems in HD, and hydrophones for bioacoustics studies. Moreover, information about anthropogenic impact data (tourism activities, fishery practices, habitat degradation, marine litter, marine traffic) were also collected.


Bari

The Youth Camps in Bari was held from October 7th to October 9th 2021. The JDC team implemented 3 days of Youth Camps in total, involving 12 youth with autism, people with disabilities and their support staff. Unfortunately, due to adverse weather conditions dolphin watching and citizen science activities at sea were carried out only once on 7th October.
In Bari, two local stakeholders were actively involved in the activities:

  • Cooperativa Dalla Luna ONLUS - organization looking after people with autism or with disabilities involving them with projects for their social inclusion
  • Plana Sailing - sailing school

Termoli

The Youth Camps in Termoli took place on 13th October and 14th October 2021. The persisting adverse weather conditions did not allow “Il Porto di Taranto” to reach the venue. With the important support of the project partner, the Municipality of Termoli, two days of Youth Camps were organized in the harbour area. The JDC experts and the external expert of the Municipality of Termoli, Doctor Antonio Celona, implemented awareness and educational workshops to let young participants discover how citizen science projects work for the preservation of cetacean species. Moreover, on both days, the young participants could experience the Virtual Reality carried out by the Lead Partner giving the chance to young participants to discover thanks to the latest technological support, the world of the oceans.


Kotor

The Youth Camps in Kotor took place from 18th October to 20th October, 2021. Lead Partner working side by side with the Project Partner Tehnopolis implemented 3 days of Youth Camps activities involving 16 young people (the same group was involved during the three days of activities), and thanks to the support of the local partner Univerzitet Crne Gore – Institut za biologiju mora.


Vlorë

The Youth Camps in Vlorë took place from 26th October to 28th October, 2021. Lead Partner implemented 3 days of Youth Camps working side by side with the project partner Albanian Tourism Agency. In each day of activity, JDC experts shared with guests on board the principles of the Interreg projects and gave the chance to the participants to take concretely part in the research activities, using the scientific equipment on board. Moreover, early in the afternoon of the last day, before the departure of the JDC Research Vessel from Vlorë to Taranto, young participants could experience the Virtual Reality to discover more about marine biology.

Sightings

During the project activities occurred 6 sightings of cetaceans: 3 sightings during the activities of the Youth Camps and 3 sightings during the transfers from one venue the following. Three species were sighted: striped dolphin (Stenella coeruleoalba), bottlenose dolphin (Tursiops truncatus) and short-beaked common dolphin (Delphinus delphis).
The observations and studies on the animals observed took place by actively involving participants of the Youth Camp activities, using the research instruments "Il Porto di Taranto" such as binoculars, hydrophones (PAM - passive acoustic monitoring and directional hydrophone), drones and cameras equipped with telephoto lenses.

Proposals for the future

The important number of sightings, the preliminary scientific data collected relating to the presence of cetaceans in the areas explored, the active participation of young people and the involvement of local stakeholders demonstrates the concrete possibility of duplicating the sustainable tourism model that combines the study and protection of cetaceans and the marine ecosystem, thus achieving the goal of the" BioTourS "project - BIOdiversity and TOURism Strategy to protect cetaceans. It is important to underline that replicating such model is articulated. It is not enough to "copy and paste" what over the years Jonian Dolphin Conservation has achieves in the Gulf of Taranto. The reason is simple, so far the successful achievements of LP are based on Citizen Science actions in other words involving citizens, students and tourists in monitoring free cetaceans in their natural environment. Such actions are strongly linked to the territory where the JDC is based that is Taranto and its Gulf, in the Ionian Sea. To achieve such goal it is necessary to carry out a detailed analysis of the territory, considering the socio-cultural context of the study area and its geographical and naturalistic peculiarities. On one hand proving the presence of cetaceans in the project areas of interest is not enough. This challenge is not intended to discourage a future attempt to replicate the study case because, as mentioned, the potential of duplicating it, as the data collected shows, is very concrete. On the other hand, this path of analysis aims to adapt the LP’s activities to the best in relation to the area of interest, taking into consideration a multiplicity of factors affecting both scientific, technical, economic and socio-cultural fabric of the study area.
