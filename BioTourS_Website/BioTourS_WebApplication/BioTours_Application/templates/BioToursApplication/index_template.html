Project Summary

BioTourS aims at introducing best practices in sustainable cross-border tourism, tackling new challenges in environmental and marine safeguard as well as preserving the natural heritage of the Adriatic-Ionian area. The main goal is to involve youngsters in cross-border touristic activities, so as to raise awareness on cetacean conservation. By combining high technological tools with standardized survey techniques and direct involvement of youth in Citizen Science activities, BioTourS will improve knowledge on cetaceans which is currently heterogeneous and defective in the programme area. The main output is the creation of a Citizen Science tourism model, for expanding science knowledge on dolphin conservation and tourism management.... Read more

Activities

Youth camps activities were carried out over a 25-day period (from 4th October to 29th October) where four target habitats were identified: the Adriatic coast of Bari; Termoli in Italy; the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro and Albanian coastlines. The camps focused on retrieving information (abundance, behaviour, group size, sighting location) on cetaceans using thematic equipment such as echo sounders, photo-video recording systems in HD, and hydrophones for bioacoustics studies. Moreover, information about anthropogenic impact data (tourism activities, fishery practices, habitat degradation, marine litter, marine traffic) were also collected... Read more

Sightings

During the project activities occurred 6 sightings of cetaceans: 3 sightings during the activities of the Youth Camps and 3 sightings during the transfers from one venue the following. Three species were sighted: striped dolphin (Stenella coeruleoalba), bottlenose dolphin (Tursiops truncatus) and short-beaked common dolphin (Delphinus delphis). The observations and studies on the animals observed took place by actively involving participants of the Youth Camp activities, using the research instruments "Il Porto di Taranto" such as binoculars, hydrophones (PAM - passive acoustic monitoring and directional hydrophone), drones and cameras equipped with telephoto lenses... Proposals for the future

The important number of sightings, the preliminary scientific data collected relating to the presence of cetaceans in the areas explored, the active participation of young people and the involvement of local stakeholders demonstrates the concrete possibility of duplicating the sustainable tourism model that combines the study and protection of cetaceans and the marine ecosystem, thus achieving the goal of the" BioTourS "project - BIOdiversity and TOURism Strategy to protect cetaceans. It is important to underline that replicating such model is articulated. It is not enough to "copy and paste" what over the years Jonian Dolphin Conservation has achieves in the Gulf of Taranto.
